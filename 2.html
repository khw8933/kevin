Motivation

- This is the fundamentals of flying (straight and level, turns, climbs, and descents)

Threshold Knowledge Test

- What do you remember from the last flight? - Ask questions about the primary control surfaces (ailerons, elevators, and rudder) and what happens to the primary control surfaces when we move the control column and the rudder pedals

Essential Background Knowledge

- Definition: VFR , Horizon(main reference & the line separates ground form sky), Attitudes & Movements , Cowling(Nose of aircraft)

Movements: motion of the aircraft around 3 axes





  1. Pitch: around the lateral axis/ achieved by elevator (forward&back pressure)
  2. Roll: around the longitudinal axis/ achieved by aileron (control column left/right)
  3. Yaw: around the vertical(normal) axis/ achieved by rudder (rudder pedal left/right)


Attitudes: position of the aircraft relative to the horizon

  1. Cruise attitude: wings are level (parallel to the horizon) & nose is slightly below the horizon (3finger gap)
    2. (used for straight and level flight, X-C, most of flying, and transition to flare)
  2. Nose-up attitude: the nose is higher than cruise and the nose touches the horizon
    3. (used for takeoff, climb, and flare)
  3. Nose-down attitude: the nose is lower than cruise (7 fingers gap below the horizon)
    4. (used for descending)
  4. Bank attitude: wings are not level (not parallel with the ground)
    5. (Used for turning)

Adverse Yaw:
control the yaw by rudder (Slipstream, Aileron drag)

Instruments:
  • Cruise – AI & Altimeter & VSI
  • Nose-up – AI & Altimeter & VSI
  • Nose-down – AI & Altimeter & VSI
  • Bank – AI & HI & TC
  • Yaw – TC (step on the ball -> ball center)

Combinations:
  • Nose up + bank
  • Nose down + bank

Safety
  1. Transfer of control : “I have control” “You have control”
  2. Scan for traffic: divide sky in 3sectors and scan each sector for several seconds
  3. Report traffic: clock system (ex. Traffic 2 o clock), altitude, head-on, converging, climbing and descending
