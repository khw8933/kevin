Slow Flight

Objective:

  • To recognize symptoms of the slow flight
  • How to maneuver an aircraft during the slow flight (midpoint of the slow flight range i.e., 50kts)
  • How to recover from the slow flight

Motivation:

  • When: Takeoff (Vr=50kts, nose is high and airspeed is low) and landing (if flare high, nose is high, and airspeed decrease and low)
  • Why: aircraft is close to the ground at low speed and if the aircraft stalls, it is dangerous

Threshold knowledge test:

  • Maximum endurance speed (explain power curve)
  • Slow flight is the range between maximum endurance and stall speed
  • Vsl (beginning of green arc) & Vso (beginning of white arc)
  • Normal approach setting and speed

Essential background knowledge:

  • Symptoms of slow flight (ask what were the symptoms when a student did max endurance flight
    i.e., how was the control column, responsive or less responsive? And how was the nose?)
    1. Nose is high
    2. Control is less responsive
    3. Airspeed is less than maximum endurance
    4. Power is more than maximum endurance (power curve)
    5. Buffeting(turbulent air hitting the tail of aircraft)
    6. Stall warning horn

Procedures:

      HALT check
    1. Height – recover by 2000ft AGL
    2. Aircraft – cockpit check L->R (carb heat on/ Mixture rich)
    3. Location – non-populated area & CYA
    4. Traffic – two 90 degree turns or one 180 turn
  • Entry:
    • P – 1500RPM
    • A – nose up (aim for 50kts(midpoint of the slow flight range) / maintain level flight)
    • P – 1900RPM
    • Trim
      • (Alternate Entry from Maximum Endurance setting)
    • P – 1800RPM
    • A – more nose up (aim for 50kts)
    • P – add 100RPM to maintain level (1900RPM)
    • T – Trim
  • Maintain:
    • Straight – control yaw & wings level
      (more yaw in slow flight due to asymmetric thrust)
      (Aileron drag is more prominent when turning/ yaw opposite direction of turn -> rudder in the direction of the turn)
    • Altitude – power
    • Airspeed – pitch
  • Recover:
    • P – full (carb heat off)
    • A – cruise
    • P – cruise (2300RPM)
    • Trim

Safety:

  • HALT check
  • Low visibility due to nose up attitude (make position calls)
  • Engine heating (nose high& low airspeed& high power)
