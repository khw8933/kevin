Range and Endurance

  1. Best angle of climb (Vx) (Airspeed that can get you to the highest altitude given altitude)
    • OBJ: How to climb at best angle of climb speed
    • MOT: For obstacle clearance
    • TKT: Various airspeeds (how to increase/decrease airspeed)
    • PROC: Raise the nose more from the Vy speed
    • EBK: 54kts with 10degree flaps/ 55kts without flaps as per POH (Maximum height given distance)

  2. Best rate of climb (Vy) (Airspeed that can get you to the highest altitude given time)
    • OBJ: How to climb at best rate of climb speed
    • MOT: For any takeoff (in case of engine failure) & ATC instruction (expedite climb)
    • TKT: Various airspeeds (how to increase/decrease airspeed)
    • PROC: Raise the nose from the normal nose-up climb or Lower the nose from the Vx speed
    • EBK: 67kts (Maximum height given time)

  3. Mixture control
    • OBJ: How to lean the mixture
    • MOT: To increase range & endurance
    • TKT: Cruise check
    • PROC:
      Rotate the mixture knob (red knob) counter clockwise gently until the engine runs a little rough (or slight drop of RPM about 25-50RPM).
      You may see a slight increase in RPM before the engine starts to roughen. Then slowly enrich (rotate the knob clockwise 2-3times) the mixture until engine smooths out (or RPM increase back to original RPM)
      (POH section 4. Cruise , leaning mixture)
    • EBK: Controls the amount of fuel into the cylinder

  4. Range (distance)
    • OBJ: how to fly for range
    • MOT: Cross-country
    • TKT: Cruise check (When you do cruise check, you are set up for range according to POH)
    • PROC: POH (Range profile)
    • EBK: Range is distance (POH section4 cruise – normal cruising is performed between 55% and 75% power)

  5. Maximum Range (might not be able to complete experiment due to weather condition)
    • OBJ: How to fly for maximum range (fly the greatest distance possible with given amount of fuel)
    • MOT: Diversion & Low fuel (urgency)
    • TKT: Various airspeeds
    • PROC:
      - Cruise Check (lean the mixture)
      2300 RPM Cruise 90kts
      2200 RPM Cruise 85kts
      2100 RPM Cruise 80kts
      2000 RPM Cruise 75kts (lean the mixture)
      1900 RPM Cruise 65kts
    • EBK: Explain Drag curve in the Flight Training Manual (pg.67)

  6. Endurance (time)
    • OBJ: How to fly for endurance
    • MOT: Cross-country & Everyday flight (practice area)
    • TKT: Cruise check (When you do cruise check, you are set up for endurance according to POH)
    • PROC: POH (Endurance profile)
    • EBK: Endurance is time (POH section4 cruise – normal cruising is performed between 55% and 75% power)

  7. Maximum Endurance (Depends on the weather condition but have to get it)
    • OBJ: How to fly for maximum endurance (remain airborne for a maximum length of time with given amount of fuel)
    • MOT: Holding & Low fuel (urgency)
    • TKT: Various airspeeds
    • PROC:
      - Cruise check (lean the mixture)
      2300 RPM Cruise 90kts
      2200 RPM Cruise 85kts
      2100 RPM Cruise 80kts
      2000 RPM Cruise 75kts
      1900 RPM Cruise 65kts
      1800 RPM Cruise 60kts (lean the mixture)
      1700 RPM Cruise No longer be able to hold both altitude & speed
    • EBK: Explain Power Curve in the Flight Training Manual (pg.67)

  8. Safety:
    • Traffic (nose is high)
    • Low airspeed (dangerous)
    • Mixture control (don’t pull out all the way or rapidly)
