Slipping

Objective

To teach:
  • How to use a slip to increase rate of descent without increasing airspeed
  • How to do a slipping turn (increase ROD without increasing airspeed and also turn)
  • How to use a slip to counteract the crosswind for landing

Motivation

  • Why:
    1. if we are too high on the approach
    2. if we want to counteract the crosswind and maintain centerline
  • When: in the circuit (base & final only)

Threshold Knowledge Test

Essential background Knowledge

  • Definition (FTM.86): The bank attitude where yaw or turn is stopped or controlled
  • 3 types of slip:
    1. Forward slip
    2. Slipping turn
    3. Sideslip

Safety

  • Instrument errors caused by slipping (The slip causes errors in airspeed indications. Some of this is due to the angle of the pitot tube but most of it is due to the location of the static air hole. With the static port on the left and a slip to the left the static pressure (now partly dynamic) is increased. This causes a decrease in the indicated air speed. Your indicated air speed is lower than you are really going. A slip to the right reverses the effects and indications. Your indicated airspeed will be higher than you are really going)
  • Normal Approach -> 65kts
  • Left slip -> 60kts
  • Right slip -> 70kts
  • Turn coordinator indication
