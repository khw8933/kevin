How does it work? Essential Background Knowledge - RMI (Radio Magnetic Indicator): RMI combines indication from a magnetic compass, VOR, and ADF into one instrument Procedures ① Finding a position: read the tail ② Track IB: fly the heading where the head points to ③ Track OB: fly the heading where the tail points to ④ Intercept and track IB on a specific track: Course -> Head -> Cut Ie., if your desired track is to the left of the head of the RMI needle, your "cut" (ie, the heading you fly), will be found an an angle to the right of the needle. In still air the cut represents your intercept angle. ⑤ Intercept and track OB on a specific track: Tail -> Course -> Cut Ie., If the tail of the needle is to the right of your desired outbound track, you needle to turn left for your "cut". Again, cut is equal to your intercept angle. Safety
