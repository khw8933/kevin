  1. Pre-flight inspection:

    • After the candidate has completed the pre-flight inspection, questions relating to the flight test aircraft will be asked.
      The candidate should be able to explain what appropriate action would be taken if an unsatisfactory item were detected
      or described by the examiner during the pre-flight inspection
    • check compass card the day before and complete deviation section on the navigation log
    • put a hood in the aircraft

  2. Departure & Enroute Procedures

    (i.e., ETA, 5Tâ€™s, RETA, position report,and ground speed check)

  3. Instrument flying (Full and Partial Panel) & unusual attitude recovery & VOR

    • Various airspeed, climbing and descending at constant airspeed or rate, and turns
    • For partial panel(timed turns), 90-180 degree turns will be conducted
    • Unusual attitude recovery in partial panel
    • Finding a position, track IB & OB, and intercept specific radial using VOR

  4. Upper airwork:

    • Steep Turn (two 180turns at 45degree bank angle)
    • Slow flight (At least 90 degree turns in both directions at 30degree bank angle)
    • Poweroff & Poweron Stall (cross-controlled stall(skidding), overshoot stall, or climbing turn with high power setting stall)
    • Spin
    • Slipping (forward slip or slipping turn)

  5. Lower airwork:

    • Forced approach
    • Precautionary landing
    • Diversion

  6. Circuit:

    • landing briefing
      • types of landing
      • runway number
      • approach speed and flap setting
      • touch down point
    • Short field Takeoff and landing
    • Soft field takeoff and landing
    • Normal takeoff and landing
    • Poweroff 180 Accuracy Approach and Landing

  7. Emergency:

    • Student will be asked 3 emergencies from one of the follwing items below either on the ground or in the air


    1. partial power loss
    2. rough engine operation or overheat
    3. loss of oil pressure
    4. fuel starvation
    5. electrical fire/smoke
    6. vacuum system failure
    7. pitot or static blockage
    8. cabin fire
    9. airframe icing
    10. electrical malfunction
    11. avionics failures:
      • attitude/heading reference system (AHRS) failure
      • display failure
      • navigational aid failure
      • alerts, warnings and cautions
    12. landing gear malfunction
    13. brake failure or seizure
    14. flap failure
    15. door opening in flight
    16. emergency descent
    17. any other emergency or malfunction unique to the aeroplane flown
