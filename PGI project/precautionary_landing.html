Precautionary Landing

Objective

To teach:
  • The procedures for a landing at an aerodrome where the surface condition is unknown,
    an unfamiliar aerodrome, or an unprepared surface (use actual example CYCW and use CFS)

Motivation

Situations:
  • Weather deterioration
  • Night (darkness)
  • Low fuel
  • Mechanical problems (partial power & rough engine)
  • Sick passengers

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • Where did we get the wind information in flight?
  • Which color of the field do we look for?

Essential background Knowledge

  • Considerations:
    • C – Civilization (communications)
    • O - Obstacles
    • W – winds (in to the wind) (ATIS, Smoke, Water ripple, Trees)
    • L – Length (aprx.2500ft, half the length of the runway 07)
    • S – Surface (look for light green field or yellowish color)
    • S – Slope (level)
  • Altitude consideration for surface check (2/3 ground clearance & 1/3 sky clearance from the cloud)
    Ex) if the cloud is at 1200ft, 800ft is the altitude you will fly at to check the surface of the landing area

Procedures

  • Stabilize the aircraft (2100RPM/ 20degree flaps/70kts (C152) & 2000RPM/20degree flaps (C172))
  • Checklist
  • Pick a field
  • Plan the approach & inspection
  1. Do the 1st high pass inspection at 1000ft (COWLSS check) (start from upwind side or crossing midfield) (FTM P.122)
  2. Do the 2nd low pass inspection at 500ft (COWLSS check and overshoot)
  3. After overshoot, do 3p's
    • PAN PAN call (121.5Mhz)
    • Passenger briefing
    • Pre-landing check

Safety

  • Look out for traffic
  • No lower than 500ft AGL for the approach
