Spiral Dive

Objective

To teach:
  • Review Power-off, Power-on stalls, and incipient spins
  • Recognize the symptoms of spiral dive
  • Recover from spiral dive

Motivation

  • Why: Dangerous in both high and low altitudes (high altitude we could break the aircraft/ low altitude close to the ground and rate of descent is high)
  • When:
    • Poorly executed steep turn
    • Incorrectly entered spin
    • In the cloud
    • Circuit (base to final when we add more bank/ that’s why we use gentle turns in the circuit)

Threshold Knowledge Test

Essential background Knowledge

Definition of spiral dive(FTM P.85):

descending turn with increasing airspeed and rate of descent

Symptoms Spin Spiral
Airspeed Low and steady Hight and increasing
Rate of Descent High and steady High and increasing
Control Less responsive Firm
Turn radius Constant Decreasing
Wings Stalled Not stalled
Airplane load increase (+4.4, -1.76) POH load factor limit
G-loading on human body (heavy weight, tunnel vision, grey-out, black-out, g-loc)

Procedures

  • HALT check (height at least 3500feet)
  • Recovery (POH emergency procedure):
    1. Power – idle
    2. Wing – level
    3. Ease out of dive

Safety

  • No solo practice
  • HALT check (especially height is very important)
  • ASI
  • Pre-flight inspection (check bolts, nuts, rivets)

Why angle of bank increases as the turn continues?

A: In a turn the outside wing has to travel farther than the inside wing. Since it has to travel farther in the same amount of time it must be travelling faster.
   Therefore, the outside wing generates more lift than the inside wing, which tends to increase the bank and tighten the turn.

Why airspeed increases as the turn continues?

A: The aircraft does not have enough vertical lift to hold it up so it starts to drop which decreases the angle of attack.
    Since it is preprogrammed to maintain its trimmed angle of attack the nose automatically pitches down and the airspeed starts to increase.
