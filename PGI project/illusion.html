Illusions Created by Drift

Objective

To teach:
  • To recognize the illusions in low level
  • To cope with the illusions

Motivation

  • When:
    • Strong wind condition
    • Especially Low altitude – Circuit, Precautionary Landing, and Forced landing
  • Why:
    • To maintain a desired track

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • Crabbing
  • Skidding
  • Slipping

Essential background Knowledge

Lower altitude = more illusions
Because movement relative to the ground is more apparent when near the ground

Procedures

  1. Take-off
    • Situations - High headwind
    • Illusions - Flying too slow (when flying at Vx and when we look outside)
    • Pilot action - Lower nose
    • Danger - Fly too low
    • Cope - Fly recommended airspeed
  2. X-wind
    • Situations – High crosswind
    • Illusions – Slipping
    • Pilot action – Apply rudder outside turn
    • Danger – Obstacle/ noise abatement/ inducing a slip
    • Cope – pick a landmark and follow that heading/crabbing or delay the crosswind turn
  3. Downwind
    • Situations – High tailwind
    • Illusions – Flying too fast
    • Pilot action – Decrease power
    • Danger – Too low indicated airspeed / stall
    • Cope – Fly recommend airspeed or RPM setting
  4. Base
    • Situations – High Crosswind
    • Illusions – Skidding outward (drifting away)
    • Pilot action – More bank & Inside rudder
    • Danger – Inducing a skid
    • Cope – Pick a landmark & crabbing/ anticipate the base turn early
  5. Final
    • Situations – High headwind
    • Illusions – Flying too slow
    • Pilot action – Increase power or lower the nose
    • Danger – too fast approach speed or low approach
    • Cope – Fly recommended airspeed

Safety

  • Fly recommended airspeed or RPM setting
  • Maintain safe altitude
  • Use gentle turns
