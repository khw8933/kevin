Short Field Takeoff and Landing

Objective

To teach:
  • Take-off and land with minimum runway use
  • Calculate TO/LD distance required for a particular aircraft

Motivation

When/Why:
  • Short runway aerodrome
  • Obstacle clearance

Threshold Knowledge Test

Essential background Knowledge

  • Landing & Take-off distance from POH (normal condition 10℃ and at sea level)
  • Landing distance for Cessna 152 with normal condition (ground roll – 465ft / 50ft obstacle – 1185ft)
  • Take-off distance for Cessna 152 with normal condition (ground roll – 695ft/ 50ft obstacle – 1290ft)
  • Take the longer distance for calculation (Takeoff distance is usually longer)
  • Safety margin ( add 200ft each side for ground roll and add 400ft each side for 50ft obstacle)

Procedures

  1. Crew briefing(run-up area):
    • Type of takeoff: Short field (10° of flaps)
    • Runway in use
    • Rotation speed: 50kts
    • Climb out: Vx or Vy (with or without obstacle)
    • Go no go decision point
  2. At hold short line
    • Request back-track and short delay
  3. Take-off
    • Line-up (threshold or displaced threshold)
    • Full brake
    • Full power (confirm static power, for Cessna 152, 2280-2380RPM)
    • Release brake and rotate at 50kts
    • Vx or Vy (with or without obstacles)
  4. At 200ft
    • Increase airspeed to Vy and flaps up
  5. On downwind
    • Pre-landing check
    • Call (stop and go)
  6. On base
    • P – 1500RPM
    • A - 65kts
    • Flaps 20°
    • T - trim
  7. On final
    • Flaps 30°
    • Airspeed 54kts
  8. Landing
    • Power off
    • Main gear touch down
    • Nose down touch down
    • Brake (slowly apply to full, don’t slam on the brake which will lock the brake)
    • Pull back on the yoke for maximum braking
    • Flaps up for maximum braking
  9. For stop and go
    • Re-configure for short-field takeoff (R->L)
  10. If steeper angle approach is required due to obstacle
    • Either higher power setting to maintain higher altitude for steeper appraoch or turn base early
    • Clear obstacle at least double the obstacle height

Safety

  • Flap setting for crosswind (use less flaps)
  • Airspeed for gusty wind landing (gust factor)
