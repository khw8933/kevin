Forced Landing

Objective

To teach:
  • Review all the pre-solo exercises for studentsâ€™ local solo practice flights (slow flight, stalls, steep turns, and spiral dive)
  • Procedures to follow in the event of an engine failure
  • Demonstrate the simulated forced landing

Motivation

  • Situations: (what causes engine to quit)
    • Fuel starvation
    • Fuel cap is not properly closed
    • Primer is not locked
    • Contamination in the fuel
    • Wrong grade of fuel
    • Too lean mixture
    • Carb icing
    • Oil starvation: Low oil pressure & High oil temperature
    • Mechanical Failure (Piston or Crankshaft break due to low oil)
  • Considerations:
    • Altitude (solo cross-country recommendation 3000ft AGL)
    • Wind (ATIS, water ripples, anchored boat, trees, smoke)
    • Surface (light green or yellow field)

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • Remember PFL procedure? What do we do if an engine fails in the circuit? (ask students in the procedure section 1-3)

Essential background Knowledge

  • Types of approaches for forced landing:
    1. Circuit pattern
    2. Figure 8 (set the key points, when 1000ft or below turn to final, final starts about 1mile from the aiming point)
  • Aiming point: Beginning of the field (so that aircraft touch down in the 1/3 of the field)

Procedures

  1. Glide 60kts (for Cessna 152)
  2. Carb heat on
  3. Turn towards the field (or options)
  4. Follow the checklist
  5. Pick a field & plan the approach
  6. Cause check & Restart (L -> R) (No cranking during restart check; Mags L->R->Both)
  7. Communications:
    • Mayday call 121.5
    • Squawk 7700
    • ELT on
    • Passenger briefing
  8. Shutdown Check (checklist)
  9. Land

Safety

  • Engine warm for every 500ft (bring the power to the green arc)
  • Not below 500ft (start overshoot at 600ft)
  • Airmanship (avoid overflying house or people)
