  1. Weather briefing:

    (highlight the route, and explain the background information of the route such as planned altitude, ETE or ETA to explain the weather)
    • GFA (study the symbols)
    • AIRMET&SIGMET (study the difference between AIRMET and SIGMET)
    • METAR/TAF (include intermediate stops & every METAR or TAF station along the route) & (study how to decode METAR. & TAF especially remark section)
    • VFR Route Forecast
    • PIREP (study how to decode PIREP)
    • NOTAM (highlight only things that might affect your flight)
    • FDs (student can infer that if there will be turbulence, headwind or tailwind along the route by looking at FDs)

  2. Navigation log & Flight Plan & Weight and Balance

    • On VTA & VNC, draw a 10 degree drift line
    • Complete flight plan for the route you are given
    • Complete 2 weight and balance sheets for the route you are given and for the actual flight test

  3. Documents & airworthiness

    • Review this: Flight Test Note


    1. Airworthiness
    2. Registration
    3. POH
    4. Weight and Balance
    5. Insurance
    6. License, Radio certificate, and medical
    7. Jorney Log

  4. Aircraft Performance(Speeds, C of G, Instruments, etc,. )

    • Memorize every speed names, definition, when and that airspeed is used and the actual number of speed
    • Characteristics of having rearward Center of Gravity and forward Center of Gravity
    • Student will be asked what to do if the line on the weight and balance sheet is drawn outside of the envelope
    • How each instrument work and power source of each instrument.
    • Instrument failures (ex., what happens if Pitot tube or static port is blocked? What happens if vacuum pump is failed?)

  6. Emergencies

    • Student will be asked two emergencies from one of the follwing items below either on the ground or in the air


    1. partial power loss
    2. rough engine operation or overheat
    3. loss of oil pressure
    4. fuel starvation
    5. electrical fire
    6. vacuum system failure
    7. pitot or static blockage
    8. cabin fire
    9. carb icing
    10. electrical malfunctions
    11. landing gear malfunctions
    12. brake failure or seizure
    13. flap failure
    14. door opening in flight
    15. spin recovery
    16. emergency descent
    17. any other emergency unique to the type of aeroplane flown
