Straight and Level Flight

Objective

  • How to fly straight and level at various airspeeds
  • Relationship between Power/Nose/Yaw

Why/When

  • Traffic separation
  • ATC instructions
  • X-C (save time or fuel)
  • Weather deterioration

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • Straight and Level flight (heading & altitude)
  • Power equation (power+attitude+trim = performance)

Essential Background Knowledge

Effect of power /Nose /Yaw during S&L Flight

Power Nose Yaw
Effect ↑ ↑ ++
Effect ↓ ↓ +

Procedures

Airspeed/Performance ① Power ② Attitude ③ Trim
90kts 2300RPM Cruise Trim
95kts 2400RPM Cruise Trim
85kts 2200RPM Cruise Trim
75kts 2000RPM Cruise Trim
65kts 1900RPM Cruise Trim
  • We will see about ±5 kts changes on the airspeed when there is ±100 RPM change
  • Add power -> the nose will move up -> add forward pressure to maintain the cruise attitude -> trim (the nose is going to be slightly lower than normal cruise)
  • Reduce power -> the nose will move down -> add back pressure to maintain the cruise attitude -> trim (the nose is going to be slightly above than normal cruise)

  • To decrease airspeed -> raise the nose

  • Point of zero movement (the numbers)
