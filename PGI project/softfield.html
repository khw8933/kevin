Soft Field Takeoff and Landing

Objective

To teach:
  • How to take-off and land on a soft/rough non-paved/paved runway

Motivation

  • Why: nose gear might get stuck in the grass
  • When: paved runway (contaminated runway) / non-paved runway (gravel, grass, or beach)

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • Remember short field takeoff and landing?

Essential background Knowledge

Procedures

  1. Crew Briefing (run-up area)
    • Type of takeoff: soft field take-off (10° of flaps)
    • Runway in use
    • Rotation speed – none or whenever
    • Climb out: Vx or Vy (with or without obstacle)
    • Go no go decision point (shorter than short-field because of earlier lift off)
  2. At holding short line
    • Control column full aft while taxiing
    • Request rolling take-off or immediate take-off
    • Runway checks done here (lights, camera and action)
  3. Take-off
    • Control column full aft
    • Line-up (centerline) but continue rolling
    • Full power (no stopping)
    • Hold nose on the horizon (As we add full power, slowly release back pressure and keep the nose on the horizon)
    • Rotation – whenever (nose on the horizon)
    • Maintain the nose on the horizon (accelerate to either Vx or Vy)
    • Climb at Vx or Vy
  4. At 200ft, Vy and flaps up
  5. On downwind
    • Pre-landing check
    • Call(touch and go)
  6. On base
    • P – 1500RPM
    • A – 65kts
    • Flaps 20°
    • T – trim
  7. On final
    • Flap 30°
    • Airspeed 54kts
  8. Landing
    • Keep power – 1200RPM (land with power)
    • Flare (cruise attitude)
    • Main gear touch down
    • Nose gear touch down
    • Control column full aft
  9. Touch and go
    • Re-configure (flaps 10°, mixture in, full power, carb heat off, re-trim)
    • Repeat

Safety

  • Be careful not to bring the nose too high after lift off because lift off happens earlier than Vr
  • Floating after flare anticipated
