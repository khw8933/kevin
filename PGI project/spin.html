Spin

Objective

To teach:
  • Review slow flight full range, power-off stall and power-on stall
  • The spin entry
  • The spin recovery

Motivation

  • When: stall + yaw (circuit base->final/ departure Vx/ turns/ in the cloud)
  • Why: rate of descent is high and it is dangerous especially close to the ground

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • Power-on stall entry

Essential background Knowledge

  • Definition of Spin: stall that is aggravated by yaw
  • 3 stages of spin: (FTM pg.82)
    1. Incipient
    2. Fully developed (if you maintain inputs)
    3. Recovery
  • Symptoms of spin:
    1. Nose low (straight down)
    2. Airspeed low and steady
    3. Rate of descent high and steady
    4. Controls less responsive

Procedures

  • HALT check
    1. we will practice spins at or above 3000 feet
    2. Traffic check & communication is especially important (lower aircrafts have priority)
  • Entry(TKT power-on stall entry):
    1. P - 1500RPM
    2. A - nose up (maintain altitude)
      3. ✦ At the stall-horn
    3. Apply full back pressure
    4. Apply full rudder in the direction you wish to spin
    5. Hold input
  • Recovery: POH section 2 limitations spin recovery
    1. Ailerons – neutral
    2. Power – idle
    3. Apply full opposite rudder and hold until rotation stops
    4. Control column neutral (release backpressure until your control lock hole about to disappear)
    5. Rudder neutral
    6. Ease out of dive

Safety

  • HALT check (height is very important, always communicate with practice area traffic and if there is traffic underneath, give way to lower altitude aircrafts)
  • Check if an aircraft is certified for spin (POH limitations)
  • Explain weight and balance (normal category & utility category)
    • (All C-152 are utility category/ C-172 normal or utility category depends on the weight)
  • No solo practice allowed (school policy)
