Power Off Stall

Objective

To teach:
  • To recognize the symptoms of an approaching stall
  • How to enter the stall
  • To recognize the stall
  • How to recover from the stall with minimum loss of altitude

Motivation

  • When: Takeoff (engine failure, if we raise the nose too high -> glide 60kts) & Landing (If flare too high, pitching the nose too high and quickly)
  • Why: An aircraft is so close to the ground with very low speed and raise the nose fairly quickly. We need to recognize the symptoms and prevent the stall

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • Symptoms of slow flight
  • Entry of slow flight

Essential background Knowledge

  • Definition of stall(FTM P.75): (Show the pictures of airflow separation)
    • A stall is a condition when the angle of attack of airfoil increases beyond the critical angle of attack such that lift begins to decrease (lift still exists)
  • That angle of attack is called “critical angle of attack” = 16-18°, depends on the aircraft
  • Factors affecting stall:
    (POH- Section 1 General -> Vsl & Vso speeds based on the most forward center of gravity)
    (POH – Section 5 Performance stall speeds, show weight, angle of bank and flaps affect stall speeds)
  • Symptoms of an approaching stall (TKT slow flight symptoms):
    1. Nose high
    2. Airspeed low
    3. Control less responsive
    4. Power greater than maximum endurance
    5. Buffeting(turbulent air hitting the tail causing the vibration)
    6. Stall warning horn(5-10kts before the stall)
  • Symptoms at the stall:
    1. Loss of altitude (altimeter & VSI)
    2. Nose-drop (center of gravity is located ahead of the center of lift(pressure). The weight of the aircraft causes the nose to drop)
    3. Wing-drop (if yaw is not controlled & skidding or slipping) (one wing has a higher angle of attack than the other)

Procedures

  • HALT check (carb heat on & mixture rich)
  • Entry: (ask how to enter slow flight then explain how to enter stall)
    1. P: Idle
    2. A: Nose up (maintain altitude)
    3. T: trim (if necessary)
  • Recovery:
    •  Power-off recovery (engine failure at takeoff):
    1. A: Nose down (Glide airspeed 60kts -> a little bit lower than cruise attitude)
    2. T: Trim
     Power-on recovery (landing with power idle but power is still available to use):
    1. A: Nose down (below cruise)
    2. P: Full (control yaw)
    3. Climb back to desired altitude

Safety

  • HALT check
  • No maintain
  • Don’t use aileron when wing drops (If a wing starts to drop and you lower the aileron on that wing to raise it, you increase the wing’s angle of attack and aggravate the stall)
