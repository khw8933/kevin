Localizer Approach

Localizer Approach Objective - To learn how to perform localizer approach - To learn how to perform circling approach Motivation - Why/when: ① when ILS approach is not available (due to vertical guidance failure) ② when airport has only localizer approach Threshold Knowledge Test - VOR approach Essential Background Knowledge - Localizer approach: is similar to VOR approach with only horizontal guidance but much more sensitive therefore better accuracy (x4 more sensitive) - Circling approach: A circling approach is a maneuver initiated by the pilot to align the aircraft with a runway for landing when a straight-in landing from an instrument approach is not possible or desirable. There are two cases where this occurs ① Where the final approach course is aligned with the runway centerline by an angle greater than 30 degrees. ② Approach aligned with the runway, but not the one you want to land on Procedures ① CYXX ILS 07 with glide slop failure ② CYYJ LOC 27 approach ③ CZBB RNAV31 and circling approach Safety
