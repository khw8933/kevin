Localizer Approach

Objective

  • To learn how to perform localizer approach
  • To learn how to perform circling approach

Why/when:

  • when ILS approach is not available (due to vertical guidance failure)
  • when airport has only localizer approach

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • VOR approach

Essential Background Knowledge

  • Localizer approach: is similar to VOR approach with only horizontal guidance but much more sensitive therefore better accuracy (x4 more sensitive)
  • Circling approach: A circling approach is a maneuver initiated by the pilot to align the aircraft with a runway for landing when a straight-in landing from an instrument approach is not possible or desirable
  • There are two cases where circling approach occurs
    1. Where the final approach course is aligned with the runway centerline by an angle greater than 30 degrees
    2. Approach aligned with the runway, but not the one you want to land on

Procedures

  • CYXX ILS 07 with glide slope failure
  • CYYJ LOC 27 approach
  • CZBB RNAV31 and circle to land on either runway 07/13/25
