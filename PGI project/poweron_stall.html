Power On Stall

Objective

To teach:
  • The recognition of the symptoms of an approaching stall
  • The entry to the stall
  • Recognition of the stall itself
  • The correct recovery with minimum loss of altitude

Motivation

  • When: take-off (Vx, low speed, nose too high) , landing(power 1500rpm, flare too early or high) and
    overshoot(raise the nose too high due to ATC instruction “pull up and go around”)
  • Why: close to the ground -> dangerous

Threshold Knowledge Test

Essential background Knowledge

  • Symptoms of approaching stall with power (TKT symptoms of approach power-off stall):
    1. Nose high (higher than power off)
    2. Airspeed low (lower than power off)
    3. Control much less responsive (control yaw with rudder)
    4. Buffeting
    5. Stall warning horn(louder than power off)
  • Symptoms at the stall with power (TKT symptoms at the power-off stall):
    1. Loss of altitude (more aggressive)
      2. (Power-on stall whole part of wing is stalled causing more aggressive drop whereas the power-off stall wing tip is less stalled because of washout)
    2. Nose drop (more aggressive)
    3. Wing drop (more likely)

Procedures

  • HALT check (carb heat on/ mixture rich)
  • Entry:
    1. P – 1500RPM
    2. A – Nose up(maintain altitude)
    3. T – Trim (if needed)
  • Recover:
    1. A- Nose down (below cruise attitude/break stall)
    2. P – Full (gently) (control yaw) (carb heat off)
    3. Climb
  • If wing drops:
    1. Lower the nose
    2. Press the rudder opposite to the wing drop
    3. Add full power

Safety

  • Incipient spin – if wing drops and the nose is below the horizon, I will take over the control
  • If wing drops and the nose is still above the horizon, I expect you to recover from the wing drop
