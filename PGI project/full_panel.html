Full Panel Instrument Flying

Objective

To teach:
  • Learn how to control and maneuver the aircraft using instrument reference only

Motivation

  • Why:
    • Safety (Fog, smoke, cloud)
    • Licensing
  • Requirements:
    • PPL (5hrs of instrument time of which a maximum of 3hrs can be done in the sim)
    • CPL (20hrs of instrument time excluding PPL instrument time, maximum 10 hours of the 20 hours can be done in the sim)
    • VFR OTT (15hrs dual instrument time of which max of 5hrs in the sim)
    • IFR (40hrs of instrument time of which max of 20hrs in the sim)

Threshold Knowledge Test

  • Power + attitude = Performance (Remember this equation?)
  • (RPM Tachometer + AI = all other instruments)
  • (Control instruments) = (Performance instruments)

Essential background Knowledge

Instruments indication: (Direct/ Indirect)

Instrument Direct indication Indirect indication
Airspeed indicator Airspeed Pitch
Attitude indicator Pitch + Bank N/A
Altimeter Altitude Pitch
Vertical Speed Indicator Climb/Descent rate Pitch
Turn Coordinator Roll + Yaw + Co-ordination Bank
Heading Indicator Heading Bank
Magnetic Compass Heading Bank
Tachometer Power Pitch

Scanning:

  • Basic – scan each instrument for a few seconds
  • Radial – involves HUB (Attitude indicator), we start scanning from AI and other instruments and come back to AI
  • Selective – scan that uses particular instruments to obtain the information necessary for a particular task
EX) Straight and Level:
using radial scan technique to scan from AI to other instruments and using selective scan technique to scan for straight and level information.
(Main instruments: Heading indicator + Altimeter)

Procedures

  1. Straight & Level
    • Review Straight and Level (Cruise attitude + visual reference or landmark for heading)
    • Primary scan for straight (AI + HI)
    • Primary scan for level (AI + Altimeter)
    • Secondary scan (TC + ASI + VSI + MC)
  2. Climb
    • Entry: Primary instruments (AI + ASI + HI)
      • APT
    • Maintain: Primary instruments (AI + ASI + HI)
    • Recovery: Primary instruments (AI + Altimeter + HI)
      • 200ft below (scan technique changes)
      • 100ft below hand on the throttle
      • 50ft APT
  3. Descent
    • Entry: Primary instruments (AI + ASI + HI)
      • PAT
    • Maintain: Primary instruments (AI + ASI + HI)
    • Recovery: Primary instruments (AI + Altimeter + HI)
  4. Turns
    • Entry: Primary instruments (AI + TC)
      • Pick a heading
      • Roll
      • Control yaw
      • Control nose
    • Maintain: Primary instruments (AI + TC + Altimeter)
      • 30° to go (scan changes; HI becomes primary)
    • Recovery: Primary instruments (AI + HI + Altimeter)
      • Roll around 10 degrees before
      • Or roll out 1/2 bank angle before

Safety

  • Small corrections
  • Keep scanning
  • Gentle turn
  • During taxi: confirm if all instruments are working correctly
  • Before turning: ask “clear left/right”
